Book of the Month

I'm so glad to tell you, march is starting right now, and with that, spring is closer!. As you may like to know, i am myself a spring baby and i really enjoy this season; it's on the little things: the flowers, the sun and the smell of the air!


And, you know hoy spring can be better? Yes!, reading a book by the seaside while sipping on a piña colada, smellng the salt air and bathing in the sunlight, cheeks flushed and tan skin. Can it be any more relaxing?


So... for this month, my choosen one will be an all's favourite of several springs since their launch in 1911.


The Secret Garden

"The Secret Garden" follows the story of Mary Lennox, a young girl who is sent to live with her uncle after her parents die in a cholera outbreak in India. Lonely and spoiled, Mary discovers a neglected garden on her uncle's estate and, with the help of her newfound friends, Colin and Dickon, she begins to bring it back to life. As the garden flourishes, so do the children, who find healing and friendship amidst its beauty.


Frances Hodgson Burnett's "The Secret Garden" is a timeless classic that beautifully explores themes of growth, friendship, and the healing power of nature. Through rich storytelling and vivid imagery, Burnett transports readers into the enchanting world of Misselthwaite Manor, where the magic of the secret garden unfolds. The characters are deeply human, each carrying their own burdens and vulnerabilities, yet finding strength and solace in their connection to the natural world. "The Secret Garden" reminds us of the transformative power of love, friendship, and the simple joys of life, making it a must-read for both children and adults alike.

About the autor

Frances Eliza Hodgson was the daughter of ironmonger Edwin Hodgson, who died three years after her birth, and his wife Eliza Boond. She was educated at The Select Seminary for Young Ladies and Gentleman until the age of fifteen, at which point the family ironmongery, then being run by her mother, failed, and the family emigrated to Knoxville, Tennessee. Here Hodgson began to write, in order to supplement the family income, assuming full responsibility for the family upon the death of her mother, in 1870. In 1872 she married Dr. Swan Burnett, with whom she had two sons, Lionel and Vivian. The marriage was dissolved in 1898. In 1900 Burnett married actor Stephen Townsend until 1902 when they got divorced. Following her great success as a novelist, playwright, and children's author, Burnett maintained homes in both England and America, traveling back and forth quite frequently. She died in her Long Island, New York home, in 1924.


Primarily remembered today for her trio of classic children's novels - Little Lord Fauntleroy (1886), A Little Princess (1905), and The Secret Garden (1911) - Burnett was also a popular adult novelist, in her own day, publishing romantic stories such as The Making of a Marchioness (1901) for older readers.
